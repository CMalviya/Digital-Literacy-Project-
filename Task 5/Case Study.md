# Phishing

**Phishing** is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details. It usually occurs through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear legitimate.

## Typical Scenario
In a typical phishing scenario:
- A student might receive an email claiming to be from a bank or online service.
- The message often creates urgency, asking the user to click a link and verify their account.
- Once the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks real.
- When they enter their login details, the attacker captures the information.

## Target Audience
Students and young users are often targeted because they may not always verify the authenticity of such messages.

## Consequences
The consequences of phishing can include:
- Financial loss
- Identity theft
- Unauthorized access to personal accounts

## Importance of Vigilance
Phishing attacks are becoming more sophisticated, making it important for users to stay alert and verify sources before sharing any sensitive information.
